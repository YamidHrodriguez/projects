Vicent van Gogh

Biography


Vincent van Gogh was a Dutch painter and one of the most prominent figures of post-impressionism. He was born on March 30, 1853, in Zundert, Netherlands, and died on July 29, 1890, in Auvers-sur-Oise, France¹. Van Gogh painted around 800 paintings, including 43 self-portraits and 148 watercolors, and created over 1600 drawings¹. His first major work was "The Potato Eaters," painted in 1885¹.

He is known for his use of bold colors and expressive brushwork, which influenced many artists in the 20th century, including the German expressionists and the Fauvists¹². Van Gogh's life was marked by poverty, mental illness, and a lack of recognition for his work¹².His works were not famous until after his death. He died at the age of 37, from a gunshot wound, which is believed to have been self-inflicted¹².
